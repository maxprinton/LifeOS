1.1 The Psychological Framework

Welcome to LifeOS. Let’s get one thing straight immediately: This is not a traditional calendar. Traditional calendars are static, lifeless grids that cause "Time Blindness"—you stare at a wall of text, feel overwhelmed, and ultimately ignore it.

LifeOS is a closed-loop, privacy-first Personal Operating System. It uses a concept called Environment Design. The system is engineered to physically alter its interface to match your current commitment, acting as a digital anchor that forces your mind into the present moment. You are no longer just scheduling time; you are programming your environment to command your attention.

🎯
The "Seed Task" Protocol:
Your biggest enemy today is planning fatigue. Never attempt to plan your entire week on day one. You will crash.

Do this instead: Hit the ➕ Nueva Tarea button right now. Add exactly one thing you are going to do in the next hour (e.g., "Clear Email Inbox"). Set the time. When you finish it, click that block on the calendar to cross it off. Congratulations, you have just completed your first dopamine loop. Build your schedule block by block, day by day.

2.1 The Dynamic HUD Engines

Your workspace acts as a Heads Up Display (HUD). Its sole purpose is to ruthlessly eliminate cognitive load. The UI adapts to your reality, ensuring you only see what is actionable right now.

⏱️
Display Offset (Día Inicia): Minimizng Visual Noise
If you wake up at 8:00 AM, seeing empty cells from midnight to 7:59 AM is a mental distraction. The ⚙️ Día Inicia control alters the displayStartMins variable behind the scenes. It physically slides the entire 1440-minute grid upwards. Set this to your wake-up time. Your digital day should begin exactly when your biological day begins.
💓
The Pulse Banner: Your Digital Heartbeat
Notice the red horizontal line tracking real-time downwards. When it intersects an active block, the top banner instantly triggers a dynamic-pulse CSS animation using that category's specific hex colors.

Why this matters: If you wander off to YouTube and switch tabs back to LifeOS, seeing your screen aggressively pulsing in red (Deep Work) acts as a subliminal, biological trigger to return to your work.

2.2 Visual Progression (Gamification)

Productivity requires immediate, undeniable feedback. If you do something hard, you must be rewarded instantly. LifeOS builds this loop directly into the environment.

The HUD features a progress bar that constantly calculates Completed Tasks / Total Tasks for the current active layout. Clicking a block marks it complete, immediately advancing the bar. More importantly, the block's opacity drops and it gets crossed out.

The Concept of "Clearing Space": As your day progresses, your calendar shouldn't look fuller; it should look emptier. By dimming completed tasks, your eyes are naturally drawn only to the bright, solid blocks that still require your energy. You are literally clearing your mental space.

3.1 Power User Block Manipulation

Time blocks in LifeOS are not text entries. They are physical objects. Amateurs open the edit menu to change a task from 10:00 to 11:30. Power users never touch the keyboard; they manipulate the DOM directly.

Action The Mechanics Technical Behavior
Move Time/Day Click & Drag Body The block detaches and follows your cursor. Drops snap perfectly to SNAP_MINS = 5 boundaries. You can drag a Tuesday morning block and throw it into Friday evening seamlessly.
Stretch Duration Drag Bottom Handle Hovering the bottom edge reveals a hidden resize handle. Dragging down extends the dur (duration) property natively without opening a single modal.
Night Shifts Midnight Ghosting In other apps, tasks that cross midnight break the UI. Here, if you drag a block past the 1440-minute threshold (Midnight), the engine cleanly slices it and projects a "Ghost Block" at the top of the next day's column. They act as one entity.

3.2 The Collision Engine

In the physical world, two objects cannot occupy the exact same space. Your time is finite, and LifeOS enforces the physics of time digitally to keep you honest.

🛑
The Blockade (isCollisionAdvanced)
If you attempt to drag a block over a timeframe that is already occupied, the system's collision engine triggers instantly. The block you are holding will turn a harsh, warning red.

If you release the mouse while it's red, the move is rejected. It bounces back to its origin. You cannot double-book time in LifeOS. You are forced to negotiate with yourself—if you want to add a new task, you must shorten or delete an existing one.

4.1 The Three Memory Layers

To truly master LifeOS, you must realize that your calendar grid is just a front-end view. Behind the glass, LifeOS operates a relational database connecting tasks, notes, and AI metadata across three distinct layers of memory.

Layer Internal Variable The Concept
1. The Grid calendarState Active Memory. These are the tasks currently taking up visual space and energy in your active week.
2. 🎒 The Stash taskRegistry Cold Storage. When you click "Guardar", the task leaves the grid but its DNA (duration, category) is saved to the Inventory. Click it later to Spawn it back onto the grid without retyping anything.
3. 📝 Notes Base savedNotes The Neural Network. The global text editor. Notes are linked to task names. If you add a checklist to a task named "Deep Work", that checklist will appear every time you spawn a "Deep Work" block anywhere in the OS.

4.2 The "Clean Slate" Protocol

Let's be realistic: Plans fail. Sometimes a week goes completely off the rails by Wednesday due to emergencies, sickness, or simple procrastination. You look at a chaotic, missed schedule and feel paralyzed.

📦
Guardar Todo el Tablero (Stash All): The Psychological Reset
When you are overwhelmed, do not delete tasks one by one. Open the 🎒 Inventory and click the orange "Guardar Todo" button.

This acts as a nuclear reset. It wipes your visual grid completely clean, but safely migrates every single task into your Inventory. You can then look at your blank week, take a breath, and "Spawn" only the tasks that matter right now. You declare schedule bankruptcy without losing a single byte of your data.

5.1 Universal Knowledge Base

LifeOS is strictly offline, meaning there is no multi-user sharing. However, the system relies heavily on a concept called Cross-Layout Synchronization (Self-Collaboration).

Because notes and AI instructions are tied to the Name of a task (not the specific block of time), your layouts constantly collaborate with each other behind the scenes.

🧠
How Self-Collaboration Works:
Imagine you are in the "Vacation" Layout, and you randomly decide to add a new lifting routine to a task called "Morning Gym".

Two weeks later, you switch back to your "Normal Week" Layout. You click on your usual Tuesday "Morning Gym" task. Because the Name matches, your new lifting routine is already inside it. Your knowledge base updates globally and permanently, regardless of your current time-view.

6.1 The "Sunday Sync" Backup

Because LifeOS respects your privacy, there is no corporate cloud database. You own your JSON file. But with great power comes great responsibility. The 💻 Sync JSON Terminal is your gateway to your file system, ensuring you never lose data despite using a browser-based app.

Every Sunday, you must act as your own server administrator. It takes exactly 10 seconds:

  1. Click 💻 Sync JSON to open the Terminal emulator.
  2. Click 📂 Seleccionar Carpeta. This uses the modern FileSystem Access API to bridge the browser directly to a physical folder on your PC.
  3. Click 💾 System Sync. The app writes a timestamped LifeOS_Data_XXX.json file straight to your hard drive.
🕰️
Time Travel (Safety Load): If you ever destroy your schedule while experimenting with new routines, open the Terminal and select a JSON file from yesterday. The system is smart enough to automatically create a safety auto-save of your current disaster before injecting the past state back into your screen. You can experiment fearlessly.

7.1 AI Context Micro-Injections

LifeOS does not lock you into a proprietary, paid AI subscription. Instead, it features a BYOAI (Bring Your Own AI) Engine that acts as a flawless context-compiler for whatever LLM you prefer (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini).

To train an AI to act as a truly personalized coach, you must give it context beyond just "Task Name: Read". It needs your metadata.

💉
Injecting Metadata:
Open any task and click the ℹ️ Instrucciones AI accordion. Write specific, brutal instructions for the agent regarding this specific block.

Example: "Check if I'm doing this too late in the day, I usually have no energy for deep reading after 5 PM. Tell me to move it if you see it here."

When saved, a 🤖 icon will appear on the calendar block indicating hidden metadata is attached to this timeframe, invisible to you, but waiting for the AI.

7.2 Macro-Extraction (JSON)

What happens under the hood? The engine loops through your entire week, calculates the exact math of your durations, grabs all the 🤖 metadata you hid in your blocks, and generates a massive, perfectly formatted JSON payload along with your System Prompt. It silently copies it to your clipboard.

8.1 The Floating Event Bubble

Time blocks are for active work that requires sustained energy and minutes. But what about non-routine occurrences like "Mom's Birthday", a "Flight to NYC", or "Rent is Due"?

If you create a 1-minute time block for these, you will clutter your grid and ruin your aesthetic. Instead, use 📅 Eventos.

These render as sleek, clickable bubbles at the absolute top of the day columns, hovering safely above your schedule. They act as "Peripheral Awareness" items—you see them, you know they exist today, but they don't block your working hours.

8.2 The Repeat Math Engine

When creating a Special Event, checking the 🔄 Repetir evento box activates a background automation engine that calculates recurrence perfectly.

Interval Type The Underlying Math Behavior
Days Calculates standard day differences. Injects the bubble every X days infinitely across all future weeks.
Weeks Calculates a 7-day modulus. Guarantees the event will always land on the exact correct weekday (e.g., Every other Friday).
Months / Years Bypasses simple day-counting. It uses Date object comparisons (e.g., "Is it the 12th of the month?") to ensure leap years and 31-day months never desync your bills or anniversaries.

9.2 The Identity Predictor Formula

Because tasks share global notes based on their Name, renaming a task is a highly destructive or constructive action. When renaming a task in the editor, look at the tiny text directly below the input field.

The algorithm runs a real-time regex check against your entire database to predict how your change will alter the data graph. Learn these symbols:

Icon Formula Logic What Happens to the Data?
✨ Not in DB Creates a brand new task identity from scratch. No shared notes.
🔗 In DB + Local Edit FUSION. This block merges into an existing group, instantly inheriting its global notes and AI metadata.
✂️ Local Edit SEPARATION. Separates this specific block from its original group, making it unique and leaving the others behind.
🌍 Global Checkbox = True MUTATION. Mutates all instances of this task across every layout simultaneously. Massive global change.

10.1 The Three Managers

LifeOS contains internal "Apps" (Managers) to handle massive datasets cleanly without ruining your minimalist calendar view. Think of them as the control panels of a ship.

  • 📝 Notes Manager A dual-pane IDE for your life's knowledge. It scans your entire database and sorts notes into Active (🟢) (currently living on a layout) and Orphaned (🔴) (blocks you deleted but the note survived). Use this once a month to prune dead notes without touching your calendar.
  • 🤖 Prompt Manager A code editor to write custom System Prompts (like "Act as a brutal coach" or "Optimize my sleep schedule"). These prompts will be automatically wrapped around your calendar JSON during export.
  • 🎒 Inventory Manager A visual grid of all your stashed task blocks, showing their default duration and category. Click any item to spawn it into the current day. It's your personal deck of habit-cards.

11.1 The Layout Router

Life is not monolithic. Your routine during exams is not your routine during summer. Layouts allow you to maintain parallel universes of your schedule.

You can Create (+), Duplicate (📄), Rename (✏️), or Delete (🗑️) layouts from the top dropdown control panel. Switching between them is instantaneous and requires no page reloads.

⚠️
The Law of Global Context Preservation:
When you switch Layouts, your calendar grid empties, but your Inventory and Notes remain identical. Layouts only change when tasks happen, not what they are.

If you delete a note for "Cardio" in your "Vacation" layout, you have deleted it globally. It will be gone when you switch back to your "Normal Week" layout.

12.1 Collisions & Renaming Errors

Why did my block turn bright red and bounce back?

The Collision Detection Engine intervened. You cannot place a task on top of another task. Either the destination time is fully blocked, or you are overlapping by just a few minutes. Make space first, or use the bottom resize handle on the existing task to shrink it and allow the new one to fit.

Why did Global Rename (🌍) throw a red error?

You tried to globally rename a task like "Running" to "Gym", but "Gym" already exists somewhere in your database. The system actively blocks global merges to prevent you from accidentally overwriting your old "Gym" notes with your "Running" notes. Uncheck the global box to simply move that specific block to the Gym category.

12.2 Orphaned Notes 🔴

Why is my note marked as "Orphaned" in the Notes Manager?

You deleted the task from your active calendar, or you stashed it in the Inventory. The note still exists safely in the global database, but since no active block on the grid is currently using that name to display it, it is flagged as orphaned. It is completely safe to leave it there. If you spawn the task again next month, the note will instantly reconnect and turn green (Active).

⚠️
15-Note Limit (FIFO Pruning):
To save system resources, LifeOS enforces a hard limit of 15 orphaned notes. When this limit is exceeded, the oldest unused notes will be automatically and permanently deleted from the database.

13.1 App Storage Limits

LifeOS operates on a zero-trust, absolute sovereignty architecture. It has no backend server, no corporate cloud, and no accounts.

🚨
The Threat Vector:
All your data is stored in your browser's IndexedDB database. While IndexedDB handles much larger quotas seamlessly, it is still vulnerable to user actions.

If you clear your cache, run a system cleaner (like CCleaner or CleanMyMac), or use Incognito mode, your LifeOS database will be deleted permanently and irreversibly. There is no "forgot password" or cloud recovery.

The Solution & Monitoring: The main HUD now features a visual Storage Gauge indicating that IndexedDB is active. You must familiarize yourself with Section 6.1 (The "Sunday Sync") and use the built-in terminal to download your JSON payload directly to your physical hard drive regularly to ensure survival.
