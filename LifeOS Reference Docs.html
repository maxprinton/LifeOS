1.1 The Psychological Framework

Welcome to LifeOS. Let’s get one thing straight immediately: This is not a traditional calendar. Traditional calendars are static, lifeless grids that cause "Time Blindness"—you stare at a wall of text, feel overwhelmed, and ultimately ignore it.

LifeOS is a closed-loop, privacy-first Personal Command Center. It uses a concept called Environment Design. The system is engineered to physically alter its interface to match your current commitment, acting as a digital anchor that forces your mind into the present moment. You are no longer just scheduling time; you are programming the physics of your daily reality.

🎯
The "Seed Task" Protocol:
Amateurs plan the entire week on Sunday and crash by Tuesday. Power users build momentum. Never attempt to plan your entire week from a blank slate.

Do this instead: Hit the ➕ Nueva Tarea button. Add exactly one block for the next hour (e.g., "Clear Email Inbox"). Set the time. When you finish it, click that block to strike it out. You have just completed your first dopamine loop. Build your schedule block by block, using the 🎒 Inventory as your magazine of habits.

2.1 The Dynamic HUD Engines

Your workspace acts as a Heads Up Display (HUD). Its sole purpose is to ruthlessly eliminate cognitive load and induce Flow State. The UI adapts to your reality, ensuring you only see what is actionable right now.

⏱️
Display Offset (Día Inicia): Minimizing Visual Noise
If you wake up at 5:30 AM, seeing empty cells from midnight to 5:29 AM is a mental distraction. The ⚙️ Día Inicia control alters the displayStartMins variable behind the scenes. It physically slides the entire 1440-minute grid upwards. Your digital day should begin exactly when your biological day begins.
💓
The Pulse Banner: Your Digital Heartbeat
Notice the red horizontal line tracking real-time downwards. When it intersects an active block, the top banner instantly triggers a dynamic-pulse CSS animation using that category's specific hex colors.

Why this matters: If you wander off to another tab and switch back to LifeOS, seeing your screen aggressively pulsing acts as a subliminal, biological trigger to return to the specific work you committed to.

2.2 Visual Progression (Gamification)

Discipline requires undeniable feedback. If you do something hard, you must be rewarded instantly. LifeOS builds this loop directly into the environment.

The HUD features a progress bar that constantly calculates Completed Tasks / Total Tasks for the current active layout. Clicking a block marks it complete, immediately advancing the bar. More importantly, the block's opacity drops and it gets struck through.

The Concept of "Clearing Space": As your day progresses, your calendar shouldn't look fuller; it should look emptier. By dimming completed tasks, your eyes are naturally drawn only to the bright, solid blocks that still require your energy. You are literally clearing your mental RAM.

3.1 Power User Block Manipulation

Time blocks in LifeOS are physical objects. Amateurs open edit menus to change a task from 10:00 to 11:30. Power users never touch the keyboard; they manipulate the DOM directly.

Action The Mechanics Technical Behavior
Move Time/Day Click & Drag Body The block detaches and follows your cursor. Drops snap perfectly to SNAP_MINS = 5 boundaries. Throw a Tuesday block into Friday evening seamlessly.
Stretch Duration Drag Bottom Handle Hovering the bottom edge reveals a hidden resize handle. Dragging down extends the dur (duration) property natively without opening a single modal.
Night Shifts Midnight Ghosting If you drag a block past the 1440-minute threshold (Midnight), the engine cleanly slices it and projects a "Ghost Block" at the top of the next day's column. They remain linked as one entity.

3.2 The Collision Engine

In the physical world, two objects cannot occupy the exact same space. Your time is finite, and LifeOS enforces the physics of time digitally to keep you honest.

🛑
The Blockade (isCollisionAdvanced)
If you attempt to drag a block over a timeframe that is already occupied, the system's collision engine triggers instantly. The block you are holding will turn a harsh, warning red.

If you release the mouse while it's red, the move is rejected and it bounces back. You cannot double-book time in LifeOS. You are forced to negotiate with yourself—if you want to add a new task, you must shorten or delete an existing one. No more lying about your capacity.

4.1 The Three Memory Layers

To truly master LifeOS, you must realize that your calendar grid is just a front-end view. Behind the glass, LifeOS operates a relational database connecting tasks, notes, and AI metadata across three distinct layers of memory.

Layer Internal Variable The Concept

3. 📝 Notes Base savedNotes The Neural Network. The global text editor. Notes are linked to task names. If you add an agenda to a task named "ERP Dev", that agenda appears every time you spawn an "ERP Dev" block.

4.2 The "Clean Slate" Protocol

Let's be realistic: Plans fail. Sometimes a week goes completely off the rails due to an emergency or a sudden shift in priorities. You look at a chaotic, missed schedule and feel paralyzed.

📦
Guardar Todo el Tablero (Stash All): The Psychological Reset
When you are overwhelmed, do not delete tasks one by one. Open the 🎒 Inventory and click the orange "Guardar Todo" button.

This acts as a nuclear reset. It wipes your visual grid completely clean, but safely migrates every single task into your Inventory. You can then look at your blank week, take a breath, and "Spawn" only the tasks that matter right now. Declare schedule bankruptcy without losing a single byte of your data.

5.1 Universal Knowledge Base (SOPs)

LifeOS is strictly offline, meaning there is no multi-user sharing. However, the system relies heavily on a concept called Cross-Layout Synchronization (Self-Collaboration).

Because notes and AI instructions are tied to the Name of a task, you can treat your Notes Base as a set of Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for your life.

🧠
Automating Domesticity via Notes:
A power user doesn't just put "Morning Routine" on the calendar. They open the note for "Morning Routine" and write:
"🧺 LAUNDRY START: Load the machine and select 'AI Wash'. The machine will perform the Inertia Test while you start your ERP work block."

Because of Self-Collaboration, every time you spawn a "Morning Routine" block—even in a completely different Layout—that SOP is there. You don't have to remember how to run your life; you just follow the documentation you wrote for yourself.

6.1 The "Sunday Sync" Backup

Because LifeOS respects your privacy, there is no corporate cloud database harvesting your routine. You own your JSON file. But with absolute sovereignty comes absolute responsibility. The 💻 Sync JSON Terminal is your lifeline.

Every Sunday, you must act as your own server administrator. It takes exactly 10 seconds:

  1. Click 💻 Sync JSON to open the Terminal emulator.
  2. Click 📂 Seleccionar Carpeta. This uses the modern FileSystem Access API to bridge the browser directly to a physical folder on your PC.
  3. Click 💾 System Sync. The app writes a timestamped LifeOS_Data_XXX.json file straight to your hard drive.
🕰️
Time Travel (Safety Load): If you ever destroy your schedule while experimenting, open the Terminal and select a JSON file from yesterday. The system is smart enough to automatically create a safety auto-save of your current disaster before injecting the past state back into your screen.

7.1 AI Context Micro-Injections

LifeOS does not lock you into a proprietary, paid AI subscription. Instead, it features a BYOAI (Bring Your Own AI) Engine that acts as a flawless context-compiler for whatever LLM you prefer (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini).

To train an AI to act as a truly personalized coach or scheduler, you must give it context beyond just "Task Name". You must teach it the physics of your life.

💉
Advanced Injection: Timezone Defense
Open a specific tutoring task (e.g., "👩‍🏫 Yuriana") and click ℹ️ Instrucciones AI. Write specific rules for the LLM regarding this block.

Example: "Since Spain moves its clocks and Ecuador doesn't, this 9:30 AM slot must be defended. Remind me if we are approaching the last Sunday of March or October to shift this block, but NEVER move it without asking."

When saved, a 🤖 icon appears on the block. The AI now understands that this is a Static Block tied to an international timezone, and it will stop "hallucinating" schedule optimizations that ruin your live classes.

7.2 Macro-Extraction (JSON)

Once your metadata is injected, you extract your human-readable calendar into machine-readable code.

  • Go to 🤖 AI Prompts.
  • Select a custom agent (e.g., "Productivity Analyst" or "Mermaid.js Gantt Generator").
  • Hit "Ejecutar Prompt".

What happens under the hood? The engine loops through your week, calculates durations, grabs all the 🤖 metadata hidden in your blocks, and generates a massive, perfectly formatted JSON payload along with your System Prompt. It silently copies it to your clipboard.

Paste that block into ChatGPT/Claude. Because you fed it structured JSON rather than a messy text summary, the AI's ability to analyze patterns, generate Mermaid charts, or critique your routine becomes terrifyingly accurate.

7.3 The Sandbox Architecture

Never let an AI directly edit your primary life schedule. A true power user isolates variables.

🧪
The "Read-Only" vs "Sandbox" Loop:
1. Keep your main layout strictly named something like DEFAULT (AI ONLY READ).
2. Use the Layout Router to duplicate it and name the copy SANDBOX (AI CAN EDIT).
3. Run your Prompt Extraction from the Sandbox.
4. Tell the LLM: "Optimize my deep work blocks in this JSON, but respect the static teaching blocks. Return ONLY the updated JSON for the SANDBOX layout."
5. Paste the LLM's output directly into the 💻 Sync JSON Terminal and hit Apply.

You can now visually review the AI's suggestions in the Sandbox layout without corrupting your real, execution-ready Default layout.

8.1 The Floating Event Bubble

Time blocks are for active work that requires sustained energy and minutes. But what about non-routine occurrences like "Pay Internet Bill" or "3 Year Anniversary"?

If you create a 1-minute time block for these, you will clutter your grid and ruin your aesthetic. Instead, use 📅 Eventos.

These render as sleek, clickable bubbles hovering safely above your schedule in the day headers. They act as "Peripheral Awareness" items—you see them, you know they exist today, but they don't block your working hours.

8.2 The Repeat Math Engine

When creating a Special Event, checking the 🔄 Repetir evento box activates a background automation engine that calculates recurrence flawlessly.

Interval Type The Underlying Math Behavior
Days Calculates standard day differences. Injects the bubble every X days infinitely across all future weeks.
Weeks Calculates a 7-day modulus. Guarantees the event will always land on the exact correct weekday (e.g., Every other Friday).
Months / Years Bypasses simple day-counting. It uses Date object comparisons (e.g., "Is it the 12th of the month?") to ensure leap years and 31-day months never desync your bills or anniversaries.

The algorithm runs a real-time regex check against your entire database to predict how your change will alter the data graph. Learn these symbols:

🔗 In DB + Local Edit FUSION. This block merges into an existing group, instantly inheriting its global notes and AI metadata.

10.1 The Three Managers

LifeOS contains internal "Apps" (Managers) to handle massive datasets cleanly without ruining your minimalist calendar view. Think of them as the control panels of a ship.

  • 📝 Notes Manager A dual-pane IDE for your life's knowledge. It scans your entire database and sorts notes into Active (🟢) (currently living on a layout) and Orphaned (🔴) (blocks you deleted but the note survived). Prune dead notes here without touching your calendar.
  • 🤖 Prompt Manager A code editor to write custom System Prompts for your LLMs. These prompts automatically wrap around your calendar JSON data during export.
  • 🎒 Inventory Manager A visual grid of all your stashed task blocks, showing their default duration and category. Click any item to spawn it into the current day. It's your personal deck of habit-cards.

11.1 The Layout Router

Life is not monolithic. Your routine during exams is not your routine during summer. Layouts allow you to maintain parallel universes of your schedule.

You can Create (+), Duplicate (📄), Rename (✏️), or Delete (🗑️) layouts from the top dropdown control panel. Switching between them is instantaneous and requires no page reloads.

⚠️
The Law of Global Context Preservation:
When you switch Layouts, your calendar grid empties, but your Inventory and Notes remain identical. Layouts only change when tasks happen, not what they are.

If you delete a note for "Cardio" in your "Sandbox" layout, you have deleted it globally. It will be gone when you switch back to your "Default" layout.

12.1 Collisions & Renaming Errors

Why did my block turn bright red and bounce back?

The Collision Detection Engine intervened. You cannot place a task on top of another task. Make space first, or use the bottom resize handle on the existing task to shrink it and allow the new one to fit. You cannot bend the physics of time.

Why did Global Rename (🌍) throw a red error?

You tried to globally rename a task like "Running" to "Gym", but "Gym" already exists somewhere in your database. The system actively blocks global merges to prevent you from accidentally overwriting your old "Gym" notes with your "Running" notes. Uncheck the global box to simply move that specific block to the Gym category.

12.2 Orphaned Notes 🔴

Why is my note marked as "Orphaned" in the Notes Manager?

You deleted the task from your active calendar, or you stashed it in the Inventory. The note still exists safely in the global database, but since no active block on the grid is currently using that name to display it, it is flagged as orphaned. It is completely safe to leave it there. If you spawn the task again next month, the note will instantly reconnect and turn green (Active).

⚠️
15-Note Limit (FIFO Pruning):
To save system resources, LifeOS enforces a hard limit of 15 orphaned notes. When this limit is exceeded, the oldest unused notes will be automatically and permanently deleted from the database.

13.1 App Storage Limits

LifeOS operates on a zero-trust, absolute sovereignty architecture. It has no backend server, no corporate cloud, and no accounts.

🚨
The Threat Vector:
All your data is stored in your browser's IndexedDB database. While IndexedDB handles massive quotas seamlessly, it is still vulnerable to user actions.

If you clear your cache, run a system cleaner, or use Incognito mode, your LifeOS database will be deleted permanently and irreversibly. There is no "forgot password" or cloud recovery.

The Solution & Monitoring: The main HUD features a visual Storage Gauge. You must familiarize yourself with Section 6.1 (The "Sunday Sync") and use the built-in terminal to download your JSON payload directly to your physical hard drive regularly to ensure survival.

14.1 The Legends Engine

Every task block in LifeOS belongs to a Category (internally called a "Legend"). This is not cosmetic decoration—it is a metadata classification layer that drives the entire behavioral engine of the application.

Click 🏷️ Categorías in the control bar to open the Legends Editor. Each legend is defined by three properties:

Property Internal Variable What It Controls
Name label The human-readable identity of the category (e.g., "Deep Work", "Commute", "ESL Teaching"). This is displayed in the legend bar, the task editor dropdown, and the AI JSON export.
Background Color bg The fill color of every block assigned to this category. This hex value is also injected into the dynamic-pulse CSS animation and the full-body background morph when the time-line crosses an active task.
Border Color border The left-edge accent stripe on the block. Used for rapid visual scanning—your peripheral vision distinguishes categories by this stripe before reading any text.
🧬
Legends Are Global Metadata:
When you assign a task to a category, that classification persists across all layouts, the Inventory, and the AI JSON export. If you tell your LLM to "optimize my Deep Work blocks but never touch my Teaching blocks," the AI can parse this because each block in the JSON carries its type field—which maps directly to the Legend you assigned.

This is the key insight: Legends are not labels. They are machine-readable taxonomies that allow both you and your AI to filter, sort, and reason about your time at scale.
🏗️
Power User Legend Architecture:
Amateurs create legends named "Work" and "Personal." This is useless—it collapses all signal into noise. A power user designs legends like database schemas:

"🧠 Deep Work" — "☕ Buffer/Transition" — "👩‍🏫 Live Teaching" — "💪 Physical" — "🏠 Domestic Ops" — "📚 Learning" — "🎮 Recharge"

Each category should represent a distinct cognitive state. When your HUD morphs from the calm blue of "Buffer" to the aggressive orange of "Deep Work," your brain subconsciously shifts gears before you even read the task name. You are programming Pavlovian triggers directly into your visual cortex.

14.2 Power User Color Science

Choosing random colors for your legends is an amateur mistake. The LifeOS HUD dynamically morphs the entire page background and the pulse animation color based on whatever task your time-line is currently touching. This means your color choices have psychological consequences.

Cognitive State Recommended Palette The Psychology
Deep Focus Dark slate blues, charcoals (#2C3E50, #1A252F) Dark environments reduce visual stimulation and signal "tunnel vision" mode. Your peripheral vision dims, narrowing attention to the task.
Creative / Learning Rich purples, teals (#6c5ce7, #00b894) Cool, saturated tones are associated with novelty and exploration. They open the mind to lateral thinking without inducing urgency.
Physical / Energy Warm oranges, reds (#e17055, #d63031) Warm colors increase heart rate perception and invoke movement. Ideal for gym, commute, or household sprint blocks.
Buffer / Transition Soft grays, pastels (#dfe6e9, #b2bec3) Neutral colors serve as mental "white space." They tell your brain: this is not a performance zone. Breathe, hydrate, prepare for the next block.
Recharge / Leisure Soft greens, sky blues (#55efc4, #74b9ff) These colors reduce cortisol perception. When your HUD shifts to this palette, guilt about relaxation evaporates—the system is explicitly telling you: this time is sanctioned.
🎨
The Background Morph Feedback Loop:
Remember: LifeOS doesn't just color your blocks. It colors your entire screen. When you glance at LifeOS and the whole page is dark slate, you instantly know—without reading a single word—that you are supposed to be in Deep Focus mode. When the screen shifts to warm orange, you know it's time to move.

This is Subliminal Anchoring (Section 1.2, Pillar 3) in its purest form. You are not reading your schedule. You are feeling it.
⚡
The "Contrast Border" Technique:
Set your bg (background) to a muted, soft shade and your border to a high-contrast, saturated version of the same hue. For example: bg: #dfe6e9 + border: #2d3436.

This creates a scannable "racing stripe" effect on your calendar. When scrolling at full speed, your eyes automatically lock onto the border colors, letting you identify task types instantaneously without ever reading a label.
